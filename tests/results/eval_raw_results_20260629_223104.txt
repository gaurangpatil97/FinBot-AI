## Explanation of Mechanics  
- Negative tax expense can occur due to:  
  - Recognition of deferred tax assets (DTAs) arising from carry-forward losses or timing differences that are now expected to be realized.  
  - Reversal of earlier tax provisions or adjustments due to changes in tax laws or assessments.  
  - Tax incentives or credits granted by the government.  

- In this case, the tax credit of ₹43.07 Cr exceeded the PBT of ₹36.67 Cr, resulting in net profit being higher than PBT.

  - Validity and recoverability of deferred tax assets claimed.  
  - Whether the tax credit is supported by appropriate documentation and tax authority approvals.  
  - Potential aggressive accounting practices to inflate net profit.  
  - Consistency with tax disclosures and notes in the annual report.  
  - Any unusual or non-recurring tax adjustments that may distort earnings quality.  

- Absence of clear disclosure or explanation in the financial statements or MD&A about this negative tax could be a red flag.  

- From an audit and governance perspective, this situation requires detailed review under auditing standards (e.g., SA 701 on Key Audit Matters) to ensure transparency and accuracy.
