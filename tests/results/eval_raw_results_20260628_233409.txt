- It can also arise from tax credits, refunds, or adjustments related to prior years.  
- The tax benefit is recorded in the P&L, reducing tax expense below zero and increasing net profit beyond PBT.

- Such a large negative tax relative to PBT is unusual and should be examined carefully.  
- Potential concerns include:  
  - Aggressive recognition of deferred tax assets without sufficient evidence of future taxable profits.  
  - One-time tax adjustments or reversals that may not be sustainable.  
  - Possible misclassification or manipulation of tax accounting entries to inflate net profit.  
  - Risk of tax disputes or reassessments if tax benefits are not supported by tax authorities.
