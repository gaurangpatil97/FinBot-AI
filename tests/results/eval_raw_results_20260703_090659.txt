## Details
- Profit before tax (PBT) FY2017: ₹36.67 Cr  
- Tax expense FY2017: ₹-43.07 Cr (negative tax)  
- Net profit FY2017: ₹79.74 Cr (which is PBT + (-Tax))

## Explanation of Mechanics
- Negative tax expense can arise from:
  - Recognition of deferred tax assets due to carry-forward losses or timing differences.
  - Tax credits or refunds recognized in the year.
  - Reversal of prior year tax provisions.
- This results in a tax benefit that increases net profit beyond the accounting profit before tax.

## Forensic Concerns
- A negative effective tax rate exceeding 100% is unusual and should be investigated for:
  - Proper disclosure and justification in notes to accounts or MD&A.
  - Verification of deferred tax asset recognition and its recoverability.
  - Potential aggressive tax accounting or manipulation.
  - Risk of misstated earnings if tax benefits are not sustainable or properly supported.
- Absence of clear explanation or supporting documentation in the annual report or audit notes would be a red flag for forensic review.
