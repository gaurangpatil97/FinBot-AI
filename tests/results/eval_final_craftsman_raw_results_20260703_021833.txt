Actual:   ## Summary
In FY2017, Craftsman Automation Ltd reported a negative effective tax rate because the tax expense was negative (-₹43.07 Cr) while profit before tax (PBT) was positive at ₹36.67 Cr, resulting in net profit (₹79.74 Cr) exceeding PBT. This unusual situation arises from tax benefits or credits exceeding taxable income, which warrants forensic scrutiny for potential aggressive tax planning, deferred tax asset recognition, or accounting anomalies.

## Details
- Profit before tax (PBT) in FY2017: ₹36.67 Cr  
- Tax expense in FY2017: -₹43.07 Cr (negative tax)  
- Net profit in FY2017: ₹79.74 Cr (which is PBT + (-Tax))  
- Effective tax rate = Tax / PBT = (-43.07) / 36.67 = -117.5% (negative and greater than 100% in absolute terms)

The negative tax expense means the company recognized a tax credit or benefit that not only wiped out the tax liability but also increased net profit beyond PBT.

## Explanation of Mechanics
- Negative tax expense can occur due to recognition of deferred tax assets (DTAs) from prior losses or timing differences, tax refunds, or tax incentives/subsidies.
- The tax credit recognized may relate to carry-forward losses, investment allowances, or other tax credits.
- This results in net profit being higher than PBT because tax benefits are accounted as income (negative expense).

## Forensic Concerns
- Such a large negative tax expense relative to PBT is unusual and should be examined carefully.
- Potential concerns include:
  - Overstatement of deferred tax assets without sufficient future taxable profits to realize them.
  - Aggressive tax planning or use of tax shelters.
  - Errors or manipulation in tax accounting or recognition of tax credits.
  - Lack of transparency or inadequate disclosure about the nature and sustainability of tax benefits.
- Auditors and analysts should verify the validity and recoverability of deferred tax assets and ensure compliance with accounting standards (e.g., IND AS 12).
- The company should disclose the components of tax expense clearly in notes to accounts to avoid misleading financial statement users.
