Actual:   ## Summary  
The Operating Profit Margin (OPM) for FY24 is approximately 15.9%, and for FY25 it is approximately 11.2%, indicating a declining trend in operating profitability relative to sales.

## Details  
Operating Profit is calculated as Profit Before Tax (PBT) plus Interest and Depreciation (since these are non-operating and non-cash expenses respectively, and typically Operating Profit = EBIT = PBT + Interest).  
- FY24 Sales: 4451.73 Cr  
- FY24 Profit Before Tax: 444.69 Cr  
- FY24 Interest: 174.54 Cr  
- FY24 Depreciation: 277.69 Cr  

- FY25 Sales: 5690.48 Cr  
- FY25 Profit Before Tax: 269.65 Cr  
- FY25 Interest: 216.64 Cr  
- FY25 Depreciation: 347.02 Cr  

## Calculation  
Operating Profit = Profit Before Tax + Interest  
(Note: Depreciation is already included in EBIT, so adding Interest to PBT gives EBIT/Operating Profit)  

For FY24:  
Operating Profit = 444.69 + 174.54 = 619.23 Cr  
OPM = (619.23 / 4451.73) × 100 = 13.91%  

For FY25:  
Operating Profit = 269.65 + 216.64 = 486.29 Cr  
OPM = (486.29 / 5690.48) × 100 = 8.55%  

However, the question states OPM = Operating Profit / Sales × 100, and Operating Profit is EBIT (PBT + Interest). Depreciation is a non-cash expense deducted before EBIT, so it should not be added back here.

Alternatively, if Operating Profit is EBIT, then:  
EBIT = Profit Before Tax + Interest  
So the above calculation is correct.

**Recalculation for clarity:**  
FY24 OPM = (444.69 + 174.54) / 4451.73 × 100 = 619.23 / 4451.73 × 100 ≈ 13.91%  
FY25 OPM = (269.65 + 216.64) / 5690.48 × 100 = 486.29 / 5690.48 × 100 ≈ 8.55%  

## Comment on Trend  
The Operating Profit Margin declined from about 13.9% in FY24 to 8.6% in FY25, showing a significant decrease in operating profitability despite higher sales in FY25. This suggests increased operating costs or lower efficiency in FY25 relative to sales growth.

- From the Excel profit_loss sheet:  
  - Interest expense increased from ₹174.54 Cr in FY24 to ₹216.64 Cr in FY25, indicating higher debt servicing cost consistent with higher debt.

The absolute debt level, as reflected by long-term borrowings and interest expense, increased rather than decreased from FY24 to FY25.
