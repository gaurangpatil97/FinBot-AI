The 3-year CAGR of revenue from FY2023 to FY2026 is approximately 8.15%, while the 3-year CAGR of total assets over the same period is approximately 9.15%. Therefore, the company's asset base is expanding faster than its revenue.

## Details  

### Revenue figures (Sales)  
- FY2023 Sales: ₹5158.50 Cr  
- FY2026 Sales: ₹6568.60 Cr  

### Total Assets figures  
Total assets = Net Block + Capital Work in Progress + Other Assets (including Receivables, Inventory, Cash & Bank, and other assets)  
From the balance sheet data, total assets can be approximated as the sum of Net Block, Capital Work in Progress, and Other Assets (since Other Assets includes receivables, inventory, cash & bank, etc.).

- FY2026 Total Assets = Net Block (2945.00) + Capital Work in Progress (88.70) + Other Assets (2778.20) = ₹5811.90 Cr  

## Calculation  

### Formula for CAGR:  
\[
\text{CAGR} = \left(\frac{\text{Ending Value}}{\text{Beginning Value}}\right)^{\frac{1}{n}} - 1
\]  
where \( n = 3 \) years.

### Revenue CAGR:  
\[
= \left(\frac{6568.60}{5158.50}\right)^{\frac{1}{3}} - 1 = (1.273)^{0.3333} - 1
\]  
Calculate:  
\[
(1.273)^{0.3333} \approx 1.0815
\]  
So,  
\[
\text{Revenue CAGR} = 1.0815 - 1 = 0.0815 = 8.15\%
\]

### Total Assets CAGR:  
\[
= \left(\frac{5811.90}{4362.10}\right)^{\frac{1}{3}} - 1 = (1.332)^{0.3333} - 1
\]  
Calculate:  
\[
(1.332)^{0.3333} \approx 1.0915
\]  
So,  
\[
\text{Assets CAGR} = 1.0915 - 1 = 0.0915 = 9.15\%
\]

## Conclusion  
The asset base is growing at 9.15% CAGR, which is faster than the revenue growth of 8.15% CAGR over the last 3 years. This indicates the company is expanding its asset base at a slightly higher rate than its revenue growth.
