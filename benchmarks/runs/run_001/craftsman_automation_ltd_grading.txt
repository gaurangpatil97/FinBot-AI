### FY2026

- Interest expense: ₹308.97 Cr (from profit_loss FY2026)

- Reserves: ₹2844.81 Cr (from balance_sheet FY2025)

## Calculation

### ROCE Formula:
\[
\text{ROCE} = \frac{\text{EBIT}}{\text{Capital Employed}} \times 100
\]

Where:
- EBIT = Profit before tax + Interest expense
- Capital Employed = Equity (Share Capital + Reserves) + Borrowings

---

### FY2026 ROCE:
- EBIT = PBT + Interest = 534.30 + 308.97 = ₹843.27 Cr
- Capital Employed = Equity + Reserves + Borrowings = 11.93 + 3252.13 + 3623.48 = ₹6887.54 Cr

\[
\text{ROCE}_{2026} = \frac{843.27}{6887.54} \times 100 = 12.24\%
\]

---

### FY2025 ROCE:
- EBIT = PBT + Interest = 269.65 + 216.64 = ₹486.29 Cr
- Capital Employed = Equity + Reserves + Borrowings = 11.93 + 2844.81 + 2358.18 = ₹5214.92 Cr

\[
\text{ROCE}_{2025} = \frac{486.29}{5214.92} \times 100 = 9.33\%
\]

---

### Cost of Debt Formula:
\[
\text{Cost of Debt} = \frac{\text{Interest Expense}}{\text{Borrowings}} \times 100
\]

---

### FY2026 Cost of Debt:
\[
\frac{308.97}{3623.48} \times 100 = 8.53\%
\]

---

### FY2025 Cost of Debt:
\[
\frac{216.64}{2358.18} \times 100 = 9.19\%
\]

---

### Comparison:
- FY2026: ROCE (12.24%) > Cost of Debt (8.53%) → Company earning above cost of borrowing.
- FY2025: ROCE (9.33%) > Cost of Debt (9.19%) → Company earning slightly above cost of borrowing.
