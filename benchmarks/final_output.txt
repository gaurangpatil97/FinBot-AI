   - Types: Currency risk, interest rate risk, commodity risk.

   - Risk of financial loss due to counterparty default on contractual obligations, mainly from trade receivables, bank balances, loans, and other financial assets.

3. **Liquidity Risk**  
   - Risk related to managing short-term, medium-term, and long-term funding and liquidity requirements.  
   - Mitigation: Maintaining adequate cash reserves, banking facilities, reserve borrowing facilities, continuous monitoring of forecast and actual cash flows, and matching maturity profiles of financial assets and liabilities.

4. **Market Competition Risk**  
   - Risk from competitive pressures in the market.  
   - Mitigation: Advancing innovation, continuously enhancing product offerings, strengthening distribution reach, and reinforcing brand salience.

5. **Operational and Other Risks** (including cybersecurity, regulatory compliance, climate change, water and effluent management, biodiversity, product stewardship)  
   - Mitigation: Setting carbon reduction targets, addressing regulatory compliance through solutions like water reuse, maintaining quality standards, and fostering responsible product design and sustainability.

## Summary

The company’s key business risks include market risks (currency, interest rate, commodity), credit risk, liquidity risk, and market competition risk. Mitigation measures involve hedging through derivatives, widening sourcing bases, prudent procurement strategies, rigorous credit assessments, maintaining liquidity reserves, continuous cash flow monitoring, innovation, and sustainability initiatives.
